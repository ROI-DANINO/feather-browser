# Commercial AI Browsers

## Purpose

Capture observations from major AI-browser efforts and identify possible lessons.

---

## Perplexity Comet

Observed themes:

- AI-first browsing
- assistant-driven workflows
- browser-mediated actions

Potential lessons:

- user trust matters
- approval flows matter
- security scrutiny arrives quickly

---

## ChatGPT Atlas

Observed themes:

- browser + assistant integration
- agent-mode concepts
- Chromium-based direction

Potential lessons:

Large companies appear interested in browsers as AI operating surfaces.

---

## Dia / Browser Company

Observed themes:

- AI-centric browsing experience
- evolution from Arc

Potential lessons:

Novel UX alone may not be enough.

Reliability and clarity appear important.

---

## Google Project Mariner / Gemini Computer Use

Observed themes:

- browser task execution
- computer-use workflows
- agent interaction with websites

Potential lessons:

Browser interaction remains important where APIs do not exist.

---

## Cross-cutting observation

Most commercial efforts focus on:

- end-user experience
- integrated assistants
- browser-as-product

Feather currently appears much closer to:

- browser infrastructure
- runtime layer
- local control plane

---

## Strategic question

Should Feather compete with AI browsers?

Or should Feather become infrastructure that AI browsers and agents could theoretically build on top of?
